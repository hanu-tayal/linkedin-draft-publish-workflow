# Amazon Q From Zero To One

## Metadata
- Theme: AI product lessons
- Primary goal: Recruiter signal
- Source proof points: AWS vision pitch, 75-person org, Amazon Q Developer, `#1` SWE-Bench, `100K+` enterprise developers
- Intended audience: AI hiring managers, recruiters, senior product leaders, technical operators

## Draft

Most people talk about AI products as if model quality is the whole game.

That was not my experience building Amazon Q Developer.

At AWS, the real challenge was not just getting to strong benchmark performance. It was aligning product strategy, model behavior, workflow fit, and organizational execution at the same time.

We started by pitching a 3-year Generative AI vision for developer tools. That led to headcount and funding for a new 75-person organization across engineering, science, and design. From there, the job was not "ship AI." The job was to make sure the product solved a real developer workflow end to end.

That meant making hard tradeoffs across context handling, agent architecture, integrations, and trust. A benchmark win matters. Reaching `#1` on SWE-Bench mattered. But adoption only followed because the product was embedded into the workflows developers already used.

That is why integrations with platforms like GitHub, GitLab, and Atlassian were not side quests. They were core product strategy.

We eventually saw Amazon Q Developer adopted by `100K+` enterprise developers. The lesson I took away is simple: AI products win when model capability, workflow fit, and distribution strategy mature together.

Too many teams still optimize one of those three and assume the rest will take care of itself.

If I were evaluating an AI product roadmap today, I would ask:
- Is the product meaningfully better inside a real workflow, not just in a demo?
- Does the system earn trust when it is uncertain, not just when it is right?
- Is distribution built into the product design, not left for later?

That is where durable AI products start.

Curious how other product and engineering leaders are thinking about this tradeoff between benchmark quality and workflow adoption.
